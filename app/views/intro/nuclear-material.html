Import licensing

Applications for Nuclear Materials Import Licences (NMIL) are now handled and issued by the Department for Business and Trade (DBT) through their Apply for an import licence service. The nuclear materials Notice to Importers, published on DBT’s import controls webpage, outlines details about the new application process.

From 8 April, the service passed from ONR to DBT, who are the sole point of contact.

You will no longer fill out a paper form downloaded through the Office for Nuclear Regulation (ONR) website.

You can access the Apply for an import licence service through the link above – please remember to save the relevant URL as a bookmark.

You will need a GOV.UK One Login account to access the system. If you need any additional help with GOV.UK One Login, contact the GOV.UK One Login team.

Importers must apply to DBT for a licence to import the relevant nuclear materials with at least 30 days’ notice.

Contact details and help

If you need advice on nuclear materials import licensing or other import controls, contact enquiries.ilb@businessandtrade.gov.uk.

Import licences for other goods

If you have any queries about import licensing for other goods, refer to the importing goods into the UK: step by step guidance.

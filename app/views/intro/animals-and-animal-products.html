Guidance on importing live animals or animal products

Comprehensive rules and guidance for importing animals and animal products to Great Britain, published by the Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and the Animal and Plant Health Agency.

Guidance on importing live animals, germinal products, and animal by-products:

  • Import live animals and germinal products from the EU to Great Britain – updated 18 February 2026
  • Importing live animals, animal products and high risk food and feed of non-animal origin from non-EU countries to Great Britain – updated 28 January 2025
  • Import live animals and germinal products to Great Britain under Balai rules – updated 14 October 2025
  • Importing or moving live fish and shellfish – updated 27 April 2026
  • Import animal by-products from the EU to Great Britain – updated 29 April 2025

Guidance on importing products of animal origin, and composite and compound products:

  • Import animal products for human consumption to Great Britain – updated 22 April 2025
  • Import composite products to Great Britain – updated 16 April 2025
  • Import compound products to Great Britain – updated 19 September 2025
  • Import eggs to Great Britain – updated 30 April 2024
  • Importing or moving fish to the UK – updated 15 August 2024

Technical documents with specific commodity import requirements:

  • Import Information Notes – updated 2 December 2024

Import restrictions

Current restrictions due to animal disease outbreaks:

  • Imports, exports and EU trade of animals and animal products: topical issues – updated 8 July 2026

Risk categories

You must find the risk category for the commodity you’re importing and follow the sanitary and phytosanitary (SPS) rules for that category.

  • Check risk categories for animals and animal products imported from the EU to Great Britain – updated 26 March 2025
  • Check import risk categories, inspection rates and related rules for animals and animal products imported from non-EU countries to Great Britain – updated 1 April 2025

Licences

You may need a licence to import animals or animal products to Great Britain.

  • General licences and authorisations to import live animals or animal products – updated 14 May 2026
  • Importing non-native animals – updated 31 December 2020
  • Import animal pathogens or carriers to Great Britain – updated 26 March 2021

Import notifications on IPAFFS

You must use IPAFFS to notify authorities in Great Britain before your goods arrive.

  • Import of products, animals, food and feed system (IPAFFS) – updated 30 April 2024

Border control posts (BCPs)

Most consignments must enter Great Britain through a point of entry that has a relevant border control post.

  • Animals, animal products and HRFNAO imports: authorised border control posts in the UK – updated 1 May 2026
  • Sanitary and phytosanitary (SPS) checks at border control posts (BCPs) – updated 30 April 2024

Welfare of animals in transport

You must make sure you meet animal welfare standards when transporting live animals.

  • Animal welfare in transport – updated 14 October 2024

  • Check if you need a CITES permit to import or export endangered species – updated 23 June 2026

  • Tag, tattoo or slap mark pigs – updated 10 October 2023

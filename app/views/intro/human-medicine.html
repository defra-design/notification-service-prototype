Import a human medicine

Guidance for UK wholesalers and manufacturers on how to import human medicines, including unlicensed medicines, how to apply for a licence and how much it costs.

Import a licensed medicine

To import a licensed medicine, applicants must obtain one or more of the following licences:

  • a wholesale distribution licence (WDA(H))
  • a manufacturer’s licence
  • a marketing authorisation, also known as a product licence (MA)

If the medicine contains controlled substances, you must contact the Home Office.

Apply for a wholesaler licence (WDA(H))

A wholesaler licence is needed when you’re importing medicine from:

  • an approved country for import list (or the EEA if you’re in Northern Ireland), and then supplying it to another country, including the UK
  • a non-approved country and exporting it to non-approved or non-EEA destinations

Costs vary depending on the type of application, the number of sites and your licensed human medicines turnover. Companies based in Great Britain may also need to appoint a Responsible Person for Import.

  • Apply for a wholesaler licence

Apply for a manufacturer licence

A manufacturer’s licence is needed when you’re importing from a non-approved country for use in the UK, or for supply to an approved country list. Applicants in Northern Ireland need this licence for imports from outside the EEA.

  • Apply for a manufacturer licence

Apply for a marketing authorisation (MA) licence

You must have a marketing authorisation (MA) licence before you can sell any human medicine. A Qualified Person must certify that the product complies with the MA and with good manufacturing practice before it’s released to the market.

The MHRA inspects manufacturing sites as part of MA applications. Manufacturers who comply with good manufacturing practice are listed on MHRA-GMDP.

  • Apply for an MA licence

Import an unlicensed medicine

To import an unlicensed medicine, you’ll need:

  • a Manufacturer’s ‘Specials’ Licence (MSL) if you’re importing from a non-approved country (or from outside the EEA if you’re in Northern Ireland)
  • a Wholesale Dealer’s Licence (WDA(H)) if you’re importing from an approved country

Before you import an unlicensed medicine you will need to notify the MHRA of your intent to import an unlicensed medicine before you import it. You can only proceed with the import if the MHRA does not object to it.

  • Supply unlicensed medicinal products (specials)

Import an introduced product

Licensed wholesale dealers can only import introduced products from authorised persons in non-approved or non-EEA countries (for Northern Ireland). Introduced products do not have a UK or approved country marketing authorisation licence.
